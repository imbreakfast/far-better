Method

Organize HPI data collection around 3 logical themes: Space, Time, and Relation.

Space

1. Character

"How would you describe the pain?"

2. Location

"Where is the pain located?"
or "Where does it start?"

3. Radiation

"Where does the pain travel?"

Time

4. Onset

"When did the pain start?" or "When does it start?"

5. Duration

"How long does it last?"

6. Frequency

"How often does it happen?"

Relation

7. Exacerbating Factors

"What makes the pain worse?"

8. Relieving Factors

"What makes the pain better?"

9. Associated Symptoms

"What symptoms are related to this pain?"

Notes

2013-09-18

TL;DR — I made a few changes!

Hi! Thanks to everyone who gave feedback on Far Better! I'm thrilled folks found it somewhat useful. Based on your feedback, I've put together some changes which I've detailed below.

(One quick note: Far Better is now hosted on Github under an open source license; the link to the repository is https://github.com/imbreakfast/far-better.)

And now, lengthy thoughts ...

  • Shane Naki made a great comment about following questions about Space with those to do with Time (as opposed to Relation), and I agree 100%, so I've edited Far Better accordingly. Shane also suggests starting with Location, followed by Character and Radiation.

    With regards to Time, my personal issue is that I'm not the best at maintaining a timeline of events in my head, so I've been saving those questions for last – that's just me.

    That said, feel free to organize the themes as you see fit. In truth, as we become more experienced and knowledgable (hopefully not too far from now), we'll probably be jumping around these questions seamlessly. For instance, your natural progression might end up looking something like:

    Onset / Quality / Location / Duration / Frequency / Radiation / Exacerbating factors / Associated symptoms / Relieving factors

    Hopefully, Far Better expedites this process for you by helping to initially organize your thoughts.

  • Sample questions that were previously close-ended were turned into open-ended questions (a la Dr. Barnewolt's excellent in-class exercise).

  • The figures and analogies were helpful to me as I was thinking through this. They're kind of silly. I realize that they're not so helpful to others, so I've moved them away from the main page and gave them their own section (see Figures).

  • I think you'll all recognize that this is super low hanging fruit, but that's kind of the point. Even at an early stage of training, I don't think much stands in the way of collecting solid data in a fluid way. And the more fluid the conversation, the more comfortable patients will feel.

2013-09-12

After reading for Week 4 of MIDPR, I started work on an alternative to "Far Colder" (pg 64). Why? Because I think acronyms are the worst, and I'd like to collect HPI without fumbling over one.

So, I present to you "Far Better" – obviously, it's a work-in-progress, and I'll be tweaking it over the next few months. If you think it can be improved, that's because it probably can; your feedback is much appreciated. Thanks!
